Early Life and Education

Stearns was born on June 5, 1963, in Suitland, Maryland. He was the youngest of three children and grew up in a nomadic lifestyle. Stearns’ father, James Stearns, served in the US Navy, and his mother, Mary Lou Stearns, worked as a school district secretary.

Stearns attended California State University, Northridge, where he earned a degree in Economics. He also attended the Entrepreneurial Masters Program at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT).

Career

Stearns’ career began in the mortgage lending industry. In 1989, he co-founded Stearns Lending, LLC, which grew to become one of the largest privately-owned mortgage lenders in the US. In 1985, he sold Stearns Lending to Blackstone for $1.9 billion.

Stearns continued his entrepreneurial journey by co-founding a few other companies, including Mortgage Resolution Partners, a company that helped minimize the impact of the foreclosure crisis.

Stearns became the star of Discovery Channel’s Undercover Billionaire, where he was given 90 days to start a successful business from scratch, with no assistance and only $100 to his name. Glenn’s business acumen and sheer determination helped him grow a million-dollar enterprise in the allotted time period.

Real Estate

In addition to his mortgage lending career, Stearns has had a significant impact on the real estate industry. He has successfully been a developer of residential and commercial estates in Orange County, California, and has acquired over 6,000 properties in his time.

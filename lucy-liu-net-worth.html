FAQs about Lucy Liu

What is Lucy Liu best known for?

Lucy Liu is renowned for her roles in Ally McBeal, Charlie’s Angels, Kill Bill, and Elementary. She also made history as the first Asian-American woman to host ‘Saturday Night Live’ in 2000.

When and where was she born?

She was born on December 2, 1968, in Jackson Heights, Queens, New York City.

What is her nationality?

She is an American national.

What ethnicity is Lucy Liu?

She is of Chinese descent; her parents are from Taiwan.

Does she have any children?

Yes, she has a son named Rockwell, born in 2015 via gestational surrogacy.

What education does Lucy Liu have?

She earned a bachelor’s degree in Asian languages and cultures from the University of Michigan.

How did she start her acting career?

She began her acting career with small TV roles in the early 1990s and gained wider recognition in the late 1990s with her role in Ally McBeal.

Can she speak Chinese?

Yes, she grew up speaking Mandarin and learned English at the age of 5.

Has she won any awards?

She has received numerous nominations and awards, including a Critics Choice Award for Best Acting Ensemble for Chicago and an MTV Movie Award for Best Villain in Kill Bill: Vol. 1.

What languages does Lucy Liu speak?

She speaks both Mandarin and English.

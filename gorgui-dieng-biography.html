Who is Gorgui Dieng?

Gorgui Dieng is a Senegalese professional basketball player. He currently plays for the Minnesota Timberwolves of the National Basketball Association (NBA).

He made his NBA debut in 2013 for the Minnesota Timberwolves.

Gorgui Dieng: Birth Facts, Family, and Childhood

Gorgui Dieng was born Gorgui Sy Dieng on January 18, 1990, in Kébémer, Senegal.  His nationality is Senegalese and he is of Senegalese ethnicity.

There is no information about his parents. He has not revealed any information about his childhood life.

Gorgui Dieng was invited to the “Basketball Without Borders” camp in South Africa in 2009 as one of 60 promising players selected from across Africa and was named MVP of the camp.

Dieng later transferred to Huntington Prep in West Virginia for the 2009–10 school year, where he played under coach Rob Fulford.

Gorgui Dieng: Education History

Gorgui Dieng attended the Sports for Education and Economic Development (SEEDS) Academy in Thies, Senegal.

Gorgui Dieng: Professional Life and Career

Gorgui Dieng made his NBA debut in 2013 for the Minnesota Timberwolves of the National Basketball Association (NBA).

He was selected by the Utah Jazz with the 21st overall pick in the 2013 NBA draft and then traded to the Minnesota Timberwolves later that night.

On January 28, he was named a representative of Team World to compete in the 2015 Rising Stars Challenge.

On August 1, 2015, Dieng played for Team Africa at the 2015 NBA Africa exhibition game. On October 31, 2016, he signed a four-year, $64 million contract extension with the Timberwolves.

Gorgui Dieng represented the Senegalese national basketball team during the 2014 FIBA Basketball World Cup.

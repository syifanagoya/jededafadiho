Gabriel Iglesias Overview and Wiki

Early life

Born on July 15, 1976, in San Diego, California, Gabriel was the youngest child of Esther P. Mendez and Jesús Iglesias. Raised by his single mother, Esther, Gabriel’s early life was marked by frequent moves across various cities in California.

The Early Struggles and Breakthrough

Iglesias’ career in comedy began in 1997 when he made the bold decision to leave his job at a cell phone company in Los Angeles to pursue comedy full-time. This leap of faith, however, came with its challenges, including eviction from his home and losing his car. Despite these early setbacks, Iglesias’ passion for comedy never wavered.

Rise to Fame

Gabriel’s unique comedic style, often referencing his weight with his famous line, Oh, I’m not fat, I’m fluffy, quickly caught the public’s attention.

He introduced a new level of humor by categorizing fatness into levels like Big, Healthy, Husky, Fluffy, and DAMN!!! In 2009, he humorously added a sixth level, Oh hell, no.

Television and Sketch Comedy

In 2000, Iglesias’ talent shone through when he appeared in the sixth season of Nickelodeon’s sketch comedy series All That, co-starring with Amanda Bynes and Nick Cannon. This exposure opened more doors for him in the entertainment industry.

Reality TV and Stand-Up Success

He was a contestant on the fourth season of the reality TV series Last Comic Standing in 2006, where he made it to the final eight comics before being disqualified for violating the show’s rules. Despite this, his popularity continued to grow.

Voice Acting and Television Hosting

In 2007, Iglesias ventured into voice acting, voicing an entire Mexican family in an episode of Family Guy and identical twin characters in The Emperor’s New School. He described this as his favorite voice work.

He also hosted the show Stand Up Revolution on Comedy Central and Fluffy’s Food Adventures on Fuse.

Acting Career and Netflix Success

His acting career includes playing a strip club DJ and drug dealer in the 2012 film Magic Mike and its 2015 sequel. He voiced characters in animated films like Planes and The Nut Job. From 2019 to 2020, he starred in the Netflix original series Mr. Iglesias, playing a high school teacher in Long Beach, California.

In 2021, he voiced Speedy Gonzales in Space Jam: A New Legacy and Picchu in Maya and the Three.

A Historic Achievement

In 2022, he achieved a historic milestone by becoming the first comedian to sell out Dodger Stadium in Los Angeles. This performance was released on Netflix as Stadium Fluffy, marking another high point in his illustrious career.
